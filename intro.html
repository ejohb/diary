Background

This is the diary, written while a prisoner of war, of Robert Brown (or likely, if you're reading this, our father).

Profile

NAME: ROBERT BENJAMIN BROWN 26/01/1920-2000.

SERVICE NUMBER: 997977

ARM: ROYAL ARTILLERY. Initially 175 Field Artillery Regiment. (?)

RANK: A/Lance-Bombardier. Volunteered for the Royal Artillery in 1940.

SERVICE: Iraq, Western Desert.

Possibly 7th Indian Brigade, 4th Indian Division. 1st, 4th, 11th, 25th, 31st and 32nd Field Regiments, RA.

POW: No 31704. Listed in the German & Occupied Territories British Army Imperial Prisoners of War List.

2/6/1942 - Captured by Italian forces possibly during Rommel's second offensive against Tobruk, whilst in convoy in the Western Desert. Apparently the first they realised it was when an Italian soldier shoved a Tommy gun through the window of their transport!

Camps: Tobruk, Caserta Hospital, Nocera Military Hospital, Stalag 344 Germany.

RB had stomach issues (dysentery) and was hospitalised for certain periods. He once stated he preferred the hospital as you actually had meat rations, he remembered he once had a steak that was almost as thin as paper! Stalag VIII-B was a German Army prisoner-of-war camp during World War II, later renumbered Stalag-344, located near the village of Lamsdorf (now Łambinowice) in Silesia. In early 1945 the camp was evacuated due to the advancing Russians. RB may have been on one of the many - now infamous - long marches of prisoners towards the west, through the terrible conditions of the 1945 winter months. Eventually liberated when an Allied tank drove through the gates of his current POW camp in April 1945.

Archive and Website

The primary purpose of this project was to create an archive, as opposed to a website: i.e. simply to preserve, and transcribe the original material before the natural effects of aging etc. left too much of it unreadable and hence lost forever.

The result was a basic collection of high-quality scans of the original document and associated transcribed texts.

This website, on the other hand, is a layer on top of that archive, that allows it to be read in a convenient way. In other words, it uses the archive as assets, but additionally pairs together the scans with their corresponding transcriptions on the same page and in a natural reading order, includes additional metadata regarding interesting concepts in hover-over links, etc.

Note: because the scans on this site are drawn directly from the archive, they are extremely high-resolution, meaning they may take longer to load than those on a typical website.

Transcriptions

The vast majority of the text has been transcribed with a reasonable degree of confidence. However, some leaves have suffered significantly from wear, mould and, worse, water. Further, one folio (number 9) is missing entirely; since it would have been between two other water-damaged folios, it is possible that RB deemed it irrecoverably damaged and unfortunately discarded it.

Anyway, illegible words are currently marked with WORD. It's quite possible that with more time, image processing, or simply eyes, these sections can be further deciphered.
