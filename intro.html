What is This?

These are the dairies, written while a prisoner of war, of Robert Brown (or likely, if you're reading this, our father).

Archive and Website

The primary purpose of this project was to create an archive, as opposed to a website: i.e. simply to preserve, and transcribe the original material before the natural effects of aging etc. left too much of it unreadable and hence lost forever.

The result was a basic collection of high-quality scans of the original document and associated transcribed texts.

This website, on the other hand, is a layer on top of that archive, that allows it to be read in a convenient way. In other words, it uses the archive as assets, but additionally pairs together the scans with their corresponding transcriptions on the same page, adds navigation controls to allow a natural reading order, includes additional metadata regarding interesting concepts in hover-over links, etc.

Note: because the scans on this site are drawn directly from the archive, they are extremely high-resolution, meaning they may take longer to load than those on a typical website.

Transcriptions

The vast majority of the text has been transcribed with a reasonable degree of confidence. However, some leaves have suffered significantly from wear, mould and, worse, water. Further, one folio (number 9) is missing entirely; since it would have been between two other water-damaged folios, it is possible that R.B. deemed it irrecoverably damaged and unfortunately discarded it.

Anyway, illegible words are currently marked with WORD. It's quite possible that with more time, image processing, or simply eyes, these sections can be further deciphered.
